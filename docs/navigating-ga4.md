## Navigating the GA4 interface

Once you have access to one or more GA4 accounts, visit https://analytics.google.com/analytics/web/ and sign in.

### Finding a property
In the top global navigation, locate the property picker - a drop-down positioned to the left of the search bar. This menu displays all the organisations, accounts and properties you have access to.
Navigate to a property by selecting the organisation, account and finally the property: [Organisation name] > [Account name] > [Property name]

### Finding the Explore section
Once you've selected your property, on the left-hand menu you'll see a column of icons:
- Home – a high level overview that lacks the specific detail designers need
- Reports – pre-built, generic reports, cluttered with commercial interests
- Explore – a sandbox for custom analysis, where you can save and share your own reports
- Advertising – ad campaign reports
- Admin – setup options, aimed at administrators rather than users

Explore is the most powerful and flexible tool for custom content design analysis, and the focus of this guide. Reports built in Explore are called 'explorations'.

In Explore, you can see a list of any explorations that you've already built for the selected property, and those shared by colleagues. By default, explorations are private - you can experiment as much as you like, with no fear of breaking anything.

To create a new exploration:
1. Make sure the correct property is selected in the top navigation
2. Select the 'Explore' icon in the left-hand menu
3. Under 'Start a new exploration', select the large '+ Blank' icon (ignore the templates)

A blank exploration will open.
